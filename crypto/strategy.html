Trading Strategies

1. ICT Trading Strategy

ICT stands for Inner Circle Trader. This concept was created by Michael J. Huddleston. ICT trading focuses on how institutions such as banks and hedge funds move the market.

Main Idea

Markets move because large institutions need liquidity to place big orders. They push the price to areas where many stop losses exist and then reverse the market.

Important ICT Concepts

1. Market Structure

Market moves in patterns:

  • Higher Highs and Higher Lows = Uptrend
  • Lower Highs and Lower Lows = Downtrend

2. Order Blocks

An Order Block is the last candle before a strong move in the market. Traders expect price to return to this area and react again.

3. Fair Value Gap (FVG)

A Fair Value Gap happens when price moves very fast and leaves a gap between candles. Price often returns to fill this gap.

4. Kill Zones

ICT traders focus on high volatility sessions:

  • London Session
  • New York Session

2. Liquidity Trading Strategy

Liquidity is where many traders place stop losses or pending orders.

Types of Liquidity

1. Buy Side Liquidity

This is found above previous highs. Many traders place stop losses above highs, so institutions push price there to trigger them.

2. Sell Side Liquidity

This is found below previous lows. Many traders place stop losses below lows, so institutions push price there to trigger them.

Liquidity Sweep

A liquidity sweep happens when price breaks a high or low, triggers stop losses, and then reverses.

3. Support and Resistance

Support

Support is a price level where buyers enter the market and stop the price from falling further.

Resistance

Resistance is a price level where sellers enter the market and stop the price from rising further.

Support Resistance Flip

Sometimes old resistance becomes support and old support becomes resistance.

4. Trend Trading Strategy

Types of Trends

1. Uptrend

In an uptrend the market forms:

  • Higher High
  • Higher Low
  • Higher High
  • Higher Low

Strategy: Look for buying opportunities.

2. Downtrend

In a downtrend the market forms:

  • Lower Low
  • Lower High
  • Lower Low
  • Lower High

Strategy: Look for selling opportunities.

3. Sideways Market

Price moves between support and resistance.

Strategy:

  • Buy near support
  • Sell near resistance

Example of Combining Strategies

  1. Identify the trend
  2. Mark support and resistance
  3. Look for liquidity above highs or below lows
  4. Enter trade at order block or fair value gap

Why These Strategies Work

Markets are driven by institutional money, liquidity, and trader psychology. Many retail traders place stop losses in predictable places, and institutions use those areas to execute their large orders.
